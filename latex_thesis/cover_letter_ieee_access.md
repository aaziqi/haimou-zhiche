# Cover Letter (IEEE Access)

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, “MP-CycleGAN: Multi-Perceptual Constraints for Unpaired Underwater Image Enhancement,” for publication in IEEE Access.

Underwater image enhancement is an important preprocessing step for marine robotics and underwater vision systems, yet it is challenging in practice due to the scarcity of pixel-aligned training pairs and the domain shift across different water conditions. Unpaired adversarial translation frameworks such as CycleGAN mitigate the data pairing requirement, but they can produce unstable color mapping, structural distortions, and hallucinated textures.

In this manuscript, we present MP-CycleGAN, which regularizes unpaired enhancement using multi-perceptual constraints spanning color perception (gray-world prior), structure perception (gradient consistency), and semantic perception (VGG perceptual loss). These constraints are designed to be complementary and improve stability without increasing inference-time complexity. We evaluate the method under a unified pipeline on EUVP and UIEB, and report bootstrap confidence intervals for PSNR/SSIM to improve reproducibility.

We confirm that this work is original, has not been published previously, and is not under review elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to IEEE Access.
