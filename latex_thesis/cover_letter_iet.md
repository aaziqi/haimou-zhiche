# Cover Letter (IET Image Processing)

Dear Editor,

We would like to submit our manuscript entitled “MP-CycleGAN: Multi-Perceptual Constraints for Unpaired Underwater Image Enhancement” for consideration in IET Image Processing.

Underwater image enhancement is challenging in real deployments due to the scarcity of pixel-aligned training pairs and the strong domain shift across different water types. While unpaired translation methods such as CycleGAN reduce the need for paired data, they may suffer from unstable color mapping, structural drift, and texture artifacts. In this manuscript, we propose MP-CycleGAN, an unpaired enhancement framework that regularizes the mapping using multi-perceptual constraints spanning (i) color perception via a gray-world prior, (ii) structure perception via gradient consistency, and (iii) semantic perception via a VGG-based perceptual loss. The proposed constraints are designed to be complementary and to improve stability without adding inference-time complexity.

We evaluate our method under a unified inference and evaluation pipeline on EUVP (in-domain matched evaluation) and UIEB (cross-domain testing without fine-tuning), and we report bootstrap confidence intervals for PSNR/SSIM to facilitate reproducibility. Our results show small but consistent improvements over a strong CycleGAN baseline in matched in-domain evaluation and competitive cross-domain performance with favorable perceptual-quality tendencies.

We confirm that this manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission and agree with the content of the manuscript. A data/code availability statement will be provided in the final version.
